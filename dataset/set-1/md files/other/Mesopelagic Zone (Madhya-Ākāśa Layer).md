# Mesopelagic Zone (Madhya-Ākāśa Layer)

## Geographic Definition & Boundaries

**Depth Range**: 200-1,000 meters (twilight/disphotic zone)

**Spatial Extent**: Encompasses **85% of the ocean's volume** (1.33 × 10¹¹ km³), making it the volumetrically dominant layer. Covers entire ocean basin area but excludes trench volumes >1,000m.

**Vertical Position**: Lower boundary marked by 1% surface light level; upper boundary at thermocline base. Sunlight exists but insufficient for photosynthesis, except for Ākṣara-photosynthetic bacteria near hydrothermal plumes.

## Physical Geography

**Pressure Profile**:

* 200m: 21 atmospheres
* 1,000m: 101 atmospheres (linear increase of 0.1 atm/m)

**Temperature Structure**:

* **200-400m**: Rapid cooling from 15-25°C to 8-12°C (main thermocline)
* **400-1,000m**: Gradual cooling to 4-6°C (deep water mass)
* **Geothermal Influence**: Within 5km of Ākṣara-Vents, temperature anomalies of +2-5°C

**Light Environment**:

* **Bioluminescence Dominance**: 87% of species produce light; ambient light 0.01-1% surface
* **Violet-Gold Spectrum**: Ākṣara-induced fluorescence creates unique wavelength signature
* **Planetary Heartbeat Synchronization**: Light pulses at 0.3 Hz match core-breath rhythm

## Geological Features

**Seafloor Topography**:

* **Continental Slope**: 200-1,000m depth range, gradient 3-15°
* **Seamount Province**: 1,247 fossilized mantra-mountains rise to 200-800m depth
* **Mid-Ocean Ridge Flanks**: Mantra-Ridge slopes延伸至1,000m depth

**Sediment Characteristics**:

* **Marine Snow Accumulation**: 3-10 cm/1,000 years of organic-rich particulates
* **Syllabic Turbidites**: Event layers from coastal slumping, 10-50cm thick, deposited every 500-2,000 years

## Hydrological Circulation

**Current Patterns**:

* **Abyssal Boundary Currents**: Flow along continental slopes at 0.1-0.3 m/s
* **Seamount Wakes**: Create Taylor columns (stationary eddies) extending 500m above summit
* **Upwelling Shadows**: Behind seamounts, aged water with high Ākṣara concentration (1.5-2.5 ppm)

**Temporal Currents**:

* **40-Day Pulsing**: Ākṣara Conveyor upwelling creates velocity oscillations
* **Planetary Wobble**: 97-day polar day/night cycle drives weak meridional flow

## Biogeographic Provinces

**Deep-Mantra Forest Province**:

* **Geographic Distribution**: 500-800m depth, concentrated in Dakṣiṇa Depression (12.0 billion km² area)
* **Spatial Pattern**: Floating algae forests form patches 5-20 km diameter, drifting with deep currents
* **Light Emission**: Violet-gold glow visible from surface on darkest nights in calm seas

**Vertical Migration Corridor**:

* **Geographic Extent**: Entire ocean basin, 200-1,000m vertical column
* **Daily Cycle**: Mantra-Zooplankton ascend to surface at dusk, descend at dawn (200m vertical travel)
* **Horizontal Transport**: Strongest along western boundaries where currents concentrate biomass

**Seamount Oasis Province**:

* **Location**: Each of 1,247 seamounts creates 500 km² oasis of enhanced biodiversity
* **Endemism**: 40% of seamount species found nowhere else

## Geographic Exploration Status

**Survey Coverage**: 40% of mesopelagic zone mapped via:

* **Vetala Deep-Dive Expeditions**: Cycles 124,891-125,047
* **Kinnara Sonic Mapping**: Using mantra-echo techniques to map biomass distribution
* **Bioluminescent Tracing**: Tracking Deep-Mantra Algae movements

**Unmapped Regions**:

* **Himavantic Slope**: 60% unmapped due to ice cover and auroral interference
* **Southern Dvipa Antara**: Complex topography from submarine volcanism poorly resolved

## Special Geographic Features

**Mana-Shadow Zones**: Areas where Mana Currents block sunlight penetration, creating biological deserts 50-100 km wide.

**Prāṇa Migration Lanes**: Invisible pathways where spirits move vertically; concentrations of vent-worms and other spirit-associated fauna.
