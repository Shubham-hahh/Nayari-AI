# Mantra-Ray

**Scientific name**: *Mobula scriptura*

**Common name**: Mantra-Ray (or Scribe-Wing)

**Earliest Ancestor**: Fossilized Prāṇa-spirits of Dvipa Antara

**Type**: Syllabically Reactive

**Diet**: Plankton and "fossilized" syllables filtered from the seabed.

**Intelligence**: Moderate/Empathetic.

**Appearance**: Wide, diamond-shaped bodies reaching 5 meters in wingspan. Their dorsal side is velvet-black, while their underside is stark white, etched with natural markings that change shape.

**Behavior**: They are attracted to the sound of chanting. They will often surface near temples or pilgrimage boats, mimicking the rhythm of the prayers with their wing-flaps.

**Culture**: They act as "Mobile Shrines" for coastal Vanyastha, who believe the patterns on a Ray's belly are messages from the ancestors.

**Age**: 80–100 years.

B**ackstory**: Legend says they were originally the shadows of the first Kinnaras, which detached and fell into the sea to become living beings of rhythm.

**Reproduction**: Asexual/Parthenogenesis triggered by the *Mahā-sandhi* (12th year festival).

**Powers**: High ability to neutralize chaotic magic in their vicinity.

**Special Powers**: *Syllabic Mimicry* — Their skin patterns shift to display the literal phonemes of any mantra being spoken within 20 meters.**I**

**nspiration**: Manta Rays and Vedic calligraphy.
