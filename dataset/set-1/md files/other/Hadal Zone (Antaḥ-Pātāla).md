# Hadal Zone (Antaḥ-Pātāla)

## Geographic Definition & Boundaries

**Depth Range**: 6,000-11,200 meters (trench and depression zones)

**Spatial Extent**: Covers **2% of ocean floor area** (0.69 billion km²) but contains deepest points. Defined by trench morphology rather than depth alone.

**Geographic Exclusivity**: Only found in:

1. **Sapta-Garta Trench**: Deepest point 11.2 km
2. **Rudra's Chasm**: 9.8 km depth (polar region)
3. **Ramanuja Chasm**: 8.5 km depth (Dakṣiṇa Depression)
4. **Twelve minor trench systems**: 6,000-7,500m depth

## Physical Geography

**Extreme Pressure Regime**:

* **6,000m**: 601 atmospheres
* **11,200m**: 1,123 atmospheres
* **Compressibility**: Seawater density 1.071 g/cm³ at trench floor

**Geothermal Heating**:

* **Adiabatic Temperature**: 1-2°C at 6,000m, rising to 4-8°C at 11,000m due to Earth heat flow
* **Vent Plumes**: Superheated water (150-300°C) rises 200-500m above vent orifices before cooling

## Topographic Features

**Trench Morphology**:

* **V-Shaped Cross-Section**: Width 50-100 km at rim, narrowing to 5-10 km at floor
* **Wall Gradient**: 15-45° slopes on inner trench walls
* **Sediment Fill**: Flattened trench floors with 500-1,500m sediment thickness

**Sapta-Garta Trench**:

* **Location**: 45°S, 90°E in Dakṣiṇa Depression
* **Dimensions**: 2,800 km length, 80 km average width
* **Geologic Setting**: Subduction zone where mantra-plate dives beneath continent
* **Discovery**: Reached by Vetala Deep-Dive (Cycle 124,891)

**Rudra's Chasm**:

* **Location**: 75°N, 30°W in Himavantic Abyss
* **Depth**: 9.8 km
* **Feature**: Connects to ice caves where water freezes into mantra-crystals
* **Cultural Significance**: Considered entrance to underworld realms

Ramanuja Chasm named for mathematician, 8.5 km depth, step terraces matching partition sequences.

## Hydrological Geography

**Trench Circulation**:

* **Downward Flow**: Cold, dense water cascades over trench rim at 0.1-0.3 m/s
* **Filling Time**: 50-100 years for water mass to reach trench floor
* **Stagnation**: Residence time 500-2,000 years at bottom

**Sediment Focusing**:

* **Slump Deposits**: Massive failures on trench walls deposit 10-50m thick layers
* **Turbidite Channels**: Submarine canyons funnel sediment to trench floor
* **Accumulation Rate**: 10-30 cm/1,000 years (10-100x faster than abyssal plains)

## Biogeographic Isolation

**Endemic Fauna**: 95% of hadal species found only in single trench system due to geographic isolation.

**Population Density**: 0.01 individuals per m² (lowest on planet), but high biomass at vent sites (1-10 kg/m²).

**Pressure Adaptations**: Species require Ākṣara-reinforced proteins; cannot survive above 5,000m depth.

## Geological Processes

**Subduction Dynamics**:

* **Mantra-Plate Recycling**: Seafloor 180 million years old being consumed
* **Ākṣara Release**: Subduction liberates ancient magical energy, fueling vents
* **Seismic Activity**: Magical earthquakes (mantra-quakes) occur monthly, releasing syllabic shockwaves

**Trench Formation Rate**: Sapta-Garta lengthens at 3 cm/year, deepens at 0.5 mm/year.

## Geographic Exploration

**Survey Coverage**: **12% of hadal zone explored** (highest of any deep zone due to focused interest)

**Vetala Hadal Stations**:

* **Station Sapta**: At 11.0 km depth, 2 km from trench floor, operational since Cycle 125,010
* **Station Rudra**: At 9.5 km depth in ice-covered north, accessible only during auroral minimum

**Technological Limits**: Pressure at 11.2 km requires Ākṣara-reinforced hulls; magic behaves erratically at extreme density.
