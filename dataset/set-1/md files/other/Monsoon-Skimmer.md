# Monsoon-Skimmer

**Scientific name**: *Exocoetus varṣā*

**Common name**: Monsoon-Skimmer (or Rain-Darter)\\

**Earliest Ancestor**: *Tulsi-Seagrass*

**Type**: Seasonal Surface-Dweller

**Diet**: Aerial *Prāṇa spirits* and raindrops.**Intelligence**: Moderate.

**Appearance**: A flying fish with wings that resemble large, translucent Tulsi leaves. Their scales are the color of a stormy sky (#191C27).

**Behavior**: They spend the dry season dormant in the mud. When the *Breath Monsoon* begins, they emerge in the millions, "flying" through the heavy rain as if swimming.

**Culture**: Their arrival in Asiat signals the start of the *Annual Resonance*.

**Age**: 4–6 years.

**Backstory**: They are believed to be the "thoughts of the Tulsi-forest" taking flight to meet the ocean.

**Reproduction**: They lay their eggs in the "floating gardens" of the Kinnaras.

**Powers**: Can survive in both air and water equally well.

**Special Powers**: *Varṣā-Saṅgīta* (Rain-Music) — By vibrating their wings against the rain, they create a harmonic frequency that calms the violent *Aśva-ghāta* tides.**I**

**nspiration**: Flying fish and the Indian Monsoon.
