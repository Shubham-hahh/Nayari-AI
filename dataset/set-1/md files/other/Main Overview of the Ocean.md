# Main Overview of the Ocean

The Aryabhatta Ocean is **not merely water**—it is Divyākaśa's **circulatory system**, **memory bank**, and **breathing lung**. As the planet's only ocean, it covers **68.0% of the surface**, making Divyākaśa a water world where land exists as scattered syllables in an endless poem. Named for the Earth astronomer whose calculations accidentally described its tides, the ocean is a living entity that remembers every wave that has ever broken upon its shores and every creature that has ever swum in its depths.

## Fundamental Facts

* **Singular Global Ocean**: One connected body wrapping the entire planet
* **Surface Area**: **34.68 billion km² (covers 68.0% of planetary surface)**
* **Average Depth**: 4.5 km
* **Deepest Point**: Sapta-Garta Trench (11.2 km deep)
* **Water Composition**: Standard H₂O infused with Ākṣara particulates (0.1-3.7 ppm)
* **Tidal Range**: Up to 18 meters (three daily peaks due to Preta-Loka interference)

## The Ocean's Three Hearts

### 1. **The Physical Heart - Currents & Circulation**

* **Primary Gyre**: Clockwise south, counter-clockwise north—the planet's bloodstream
* **Ākṣara Conveyor**: Deep cold current carrying fossilized magic from poles to equator
* **Mana Currents**: Surface streams of charged particles used as navigational "light-rivers"
* **40-Day Pulse**: Synchronized with Śālagrīva's orbit, creating sacred tidal springs

### 2. **The Magical Heart - Ākṣara Distribution**

* The ocean stores and distributes magical energy
* High-Ākṣara zones glow violet-gold at night
* "Mana-Falls" occur during solar maximum—visible golden precipitation
* Acts as planetary capacitor, absorbing and releasing magical energy

### 3. **The Biological Heart - Life Support System**

* All eight continents depend on its moisture and temperature regulation
* 87% of planetary biomass resides in or depends on the ocean
* Source of the "planetary heartbeat" felt by all life (40-day wobble cycle)

## Major Ecological Zones

### 1. **Brahmapurī Shallows** (Equatorial)

* Vast delta system where seven sacred rivers meet the ocean
* Mangrove labyrinths that filter Ākṣara into wand-quality wood
* Floating gardens tended by dancing Kinnaras
* Highest biodiversity: 60,000+ documented species

### 2. **Dvipa Antara Archipelago**

* "The Shattered Isles"—nine volcanic islands in a jeweled chain
* Coral Ākṣara Reefs (fossilized Prāṇa spirits turned to stone)
* Sūrya Atolls—perfect circular islands formed by solar footprints
* Extreme endemism: each island has unique magical ecosystems

### 3. **Himavantic Abyss** (Polar)

* Deep, cold waters under perpetual aurora light
* Ice caves where water freezes into mantra-crystals
* Whalesong chambers where melodies stabilize reality
* Geothermal vents that weep liquid Ākṣara

### 4. **Mārutadeśa Marginal Sea**

* Hypersaline (4.2%) due to magical evaporation
* "Silent zones" where sound and magic dissipate
* Jalakṣaya Basin—where water syllables have been mined out
* Dangerous but rich in ghost-salt and memory-minerals

## The Final Truth

The Aryabhatta Ocean is **not separate** from Divyākaśa—it **is** Divyākaśa. The continents are but temporary consonants in its endless vowel. Every creature that swims in its waters is a syllable in its poem. Every current is a sentence in its story. When the ocean finally speaks its last word—when Ādyānta's final breath is fully exhaled—the planet will either achieve perfect harmony or dissolve into silence.

Until that distant day, the ocean continues its eternal recitation, teaching all who listen that water is not merely life, but **language**—the first and last word of a world that dreams in liquid verse.
