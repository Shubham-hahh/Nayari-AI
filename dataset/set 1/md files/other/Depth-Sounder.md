# Depth-Sounder

**Scientific name**: *Nāda-mīna\\*

**Common name**: Depth-Sounder (or Trench-Bell)

**Earliest Ancestor**: *Whale-Mantra Singers*

**Type**: Deep-Sea Sentinel

**Diet**: Marine snow and "vibrational echoes" from the planet's core.

**Intelligence**: High (Philosophical).

**Appearance**: A massive, spherical fish that looks like a brass diving bell. It has no mouth or fins; it moves by manipulating the density of the Ākṣara around it. It emits a constant, low-frequency hum.

**Behavior**: They reside permanently in the *Sapta-Garta Trench*. They act as "living beacons" for Vetala explorers.

**Culture**: They are the "Monks of the Trench," supposedly meditating on the very first syllable spoken at *The Origin*.

**Age**: 1,000+ years.

**Backstory**: They are the descendants of the first whales who dove too deep and found they preferred the silence of the core to the noise of the surface.

**Reproduction**: They split in two whenever they reach a "perfect understanding" of a cosmic truth.

**Powers**: Complete control over local gravity and sound.

**Special Powers**: *Kāla-Stambha* (Time-Pillar) — They can create a small bubble where time stops completely, preserving whatever is inside for eternity.

**Inspiration**: Deep-sea chimaeras and Buddhist temple bells.
