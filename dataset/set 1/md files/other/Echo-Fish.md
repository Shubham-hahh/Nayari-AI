# Echo-Fish

**Scientific name**: *Pisces echo*

**Common name**: Echo-Fish (or Recorder-Minnow)

**Earliest Ancestor**: Unknown (Asiat Basin origin)

**Type**: Mnemonic Parasite/Symbiote

**Diet**: Sound vibrations and magical residue.

**Intelligence**: Low (strictly repetitive).

**Appearance**: Small, mirror-scaled fish with four ears. They have no mouth for eating; they "absorb" nutrients through their scales via acoustic resonance.

**Behavior**: They congregate near places where mantras are spoken (temples, markets, courts).

**Culture**: Used by Vetalas as "Living Ledgers."

**Age**: 5–8 years.

**Backstory**: Evolved to fill the niche of "sound-recycling" in the high-noise environments of the *Brahmapurī* shallows.

**Reproduction**: Spawning triggered by loud, rhythmic percussion (temple bells).

**Powers**: Perfect auditory memory.

**Special Powers**: *Playback* — When tapped on the head, they repeat the last 108 syllables spoken in their presence with 100% accuracy.**Inspiration**: Parrots and the idea of "The Walls have Ears."
