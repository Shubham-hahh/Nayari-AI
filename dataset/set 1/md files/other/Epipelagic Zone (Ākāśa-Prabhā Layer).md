# Epipelagic Zone (Ākāśa-Prabhā Layer)

## Geographic Definition & Boundaries

**Depth Range**: 0-200 meters (surface to lower limit of euphotic zone)

**Spatial Extent**: Covers **100% of the Aryabhatta Ocean's surface area** (34.68 billion km²), though only the upper 200m vertical slice. This zone represents 0.44% of total ocean volume but supports 90% of marine primary production.

**Geographic Distribution**:

* **Brahmapurī Shallows**: Dominated by river plumes extending 150-300 km offshore
* **Continental Shelves**: 95% of shelf area falls within this zone (20-150 km from shore)
* **Asiat Basin**: 8.0 billion km² of relatively clear tropical waters
* **Mārutadeśa Marginal Sea**: Salinity-stratified surface layer, 2.0 billion km²
* **Dvipa Antara**: Complex patchwork of shallow reef lagoons and clear oceanic water

## Physical Geography

**Light Penetration**:

* **Upper 50m**: 99% of surface light, violet-gold refraction during Ākṣara blooms
* **50-100m**: 50% light attenuation, bioluminescent compensation begins
* **100-200m**: 1-10% surface light, boundary of photosynthetic viability

**Temperature Gradients**:

* **Equatorial (Brahmapurī)**: 28-32°C, stable year-round
* **Tropical (Asiat)**: 24-29°C, seasonal variation ±3°C
* **Temperate (Dakṣiṇa)**: 12-22°C, strong seasonal thermocline
* **Polar (Himavantic)**: -1.8 to 5°C, ice-covered 6-9 months/year

**Density Structure**:

* **Surface Mixed Layer**: 50-100m depth, uniform temperature/salinity due to wind mixing
* **Seasonal Thermocline**: 50-200m, where temperature drops 10-15°C over vertical distance

## Geological & Sedimentary Features

**Seafloor Composition**:

* **Continental Shelves**: Syllabic sand and limestone (coral debris) over granitic basement
* **Marginal Seas**: Fine mud with high organic content (5-15% by weight)
* **Archipelagic Zones**: Mixed volcanic sand and biogenic debris

**Sediment Thickness**: Varies from 0m (exposed bedrock on young volcanic islands) to 800m in ancient deltaic depocenters of Brahmapurī.

## Hydrological Patterns

**Current Structure**:

* **Primary Gyre Circulation**: Extends to 200m depth, forming boundary currents along coasts
  * Western boundary currents (Dakṣiṇa eastern coast): 1.5-2.0 m/s
  * Eastern boundary currents (Asiat western coast): 0.3-0.8 m/s with upwelling
* **Mana Currents**: Surface-confined to upper 50m, flowing westward at 1-3 m/s
* **Seasonal Monsoon Reversal**: Asiat Basin currents reverse 180° during summer monsoon

**Wave Climate**:

* **Equatorial Belt**: Significant wave height 1.5-2.5m, period 8-12 seconds
* **Southern Temperate**: 3-5m wave height, 12-16 second period
* **Mana Storm Events**: Can generate 8-12m waves with violet-gold bioluminescent crests

## Spatial Variations & Provinces

**Tropical-Oligotrophic Province** (Asiat Basin):

* **Geographic Position**: 15°N-35°N, 0°-60°E
* **Water Clarity**: Secchi depth 30-40m
* **Ākṣara Concentration**: 0.1-0.5 ppm (low due to stable water column)

**Equatorial-Upwelling Province** (Brahmapurī):

* **Geographic Position**: 5°S-5°N, 80°E-120°E
* **Nutrient Input**: Seven rivers discharge 12,000 m³/s of sediment-laden water
* **Ākṣara Concentration**: 1.2-3.7 ppm (highest in surface waters)

**Polar-Marginal Province** (Himavantic periphery):

* **Seasonal Ice Cover**: 60-90% in winter, creates brine rejection and deep mixing
* **Temperature Minimum**: -1.8°C (seawater freezing point)

## Exploration & Navigation Geography

**Mapped Coverage**: 100% surface charted via syllabic current mapping (Kinnara expeditions, Cycle 125,002)

**Primary Navigation Aids**:

* **Mana Current Highways**: Visible as golden-violet surface rivers at night
* **Bioluminescent Landmarks**: Deep-Mantra Algae forests at 180-200m depth create glowing horizons
* **Syllabic Wave Patterns**: Breaking waves form readable phonetic patterns for Vanyastha sailors

**Trade Routes**:

* **Equatorial Trunk Route**: Along 0° latitude, following Mana Currents from Dvipa Antara to Asiat (5,000 km)
* **Coastal Pilgrimage Circuit**: 40-day tidal cycle route hugging Brahmapurī coast
