# Mirage-Meduza

**Scientific name**: *Aurelia māyā*

**Common name**: Mirage-Meduza (or Heat-Stinger)

**Earliest Ancestor**: *Golden-Phytoplankton*

**Type**: Atmospheric/Marine Hybrid

**Diet**: Heat-syllables and sunlight.

**Intelligence**: Instinctive (Hive-mind-like).

**Appearance**: A jellyfish with a bell made of shimmering, hot air instead of water. They float on the surface of the *Mārutadeśa Marginal Sea*, looking like golden bubbles. Their tentacles are threads of pure refracted light.
**Behavior**: They congregate in "Mirage-Gardens," creating hallucinatory cities on the horizon to lure birds and sailors into the hypersaline water.**Culture**: Associated with the "Seduction of the Desert."

**Age**: 1–3 years.

**Backstory**: Born from the "Boiling Silence" of the *Agni-kṣaya* furnace, they represent the ocean's attempt to breathe in the desert.

**Reproduction**: They "bud" off whenever a traveler speaks a lie within sight of the water.

**Powers**: High ability to manipulate light and create illusions.

**Special Powers**: *Vākya-Bhrama* (Word-Confusion) — Their sting doesn't cause pain; it causes the victim to forget the meaning of the word "water" for nine hours.

**Inspiration**: Box Jellyfish and desert mirages.
