# Kinnara

**Scientific name**: Kinnara *tritiya-prakriti*

**Common name**: Kinnara

**Earliest Ancestor**: Deer-like Kinnaras of Megha Parvat

**Type**: Intelligently Harmonious

**Diet**: Varies

**Intelligence**: Depends on the environment of the organism

**Appearance**: Smooth, hairless skin with subtle iridescent sheen that shifts between golden, violet, and emerald hues under specific light. Their form is a **living synthesis** of masculine and feminine: broad shoulders tapering into a fluid, graceful frame; a strong jawline softened by high, delicate cheekbones; hands that are both powerful and elegant, with long, articulate fingers. Their eyes are large and expressive, set beneath a prominent brow that lends intensity without harshness. They stand 1.8m(minimum)-2.1m(maximum) tall, moving with a dancer's lithe grace that belies hidden strength. Their ears are elongated to shoulder-length with delicate tapered points, catching sound like a deer. Most distinctively, they possess a **feathered crest** at their crown—not static, but a living mane of peacock-hued plumes that ripple and shift color with their emotional state, a crown both fierce and beautiful.

**Behavior**: Varies

**Culture**: Varies across lands; centered in *Samanvaya Mandiras* celebrating fluid identity through sacred dance and Mithuna Path philosophy

**Age**: 140-220 years(approx.)

**Backstory**: Four centuries after "The Origin," during the Human-Vanyastha wars, deer-like Kinnaras fled the tulsi forests for the Megha Parvat highlands (a monsoon-soaked plateau). Constant exposure to Ākṣara-charged mists triggered unique evolution, manifesting simultaneous potential. The philosopher Adi-Prakriti discovered rhythmic movement could harmonize chaotic Ākṣara energy, founding the first Samanvaya Mandira. Their Mithuna Mantras resolved cosmic imbalances and mediated the Treaty of Seven Rivers. They view gender as energetic spectrum, serving as living bridges between realms and maintaining communal survival through cross-species bonds.

**Reproduction**: Kinnara reproduce through **antaraṅga sangama**—a private, unspoken merging of Ākṣara between bonded partners (2-3 individuals) across forty nights of harmonized breath and mantra. On the final night, their unified energy manifests as a **prana-bindu**, a pearl of living breath that must be planted in a sacred tulsi grove within three days. The bindu gestates for seven months, sustained by the parents' periodic meditation at the grove; neglect causes miscarriage or birth of a **Bhrānta**—a child born without ancestral memory. Cross-species unions are possible but taboo, as the bindu rejects non-Kinnara energy 70% of the time, and success requires the non-Kinnara partner to temporarily surrender their name to Mithuna—a cost many refuse. The child is always Kinnara, bearing all parents' traits in their eyes' iridescence. The community celebrates the bindu-planting, never the union itself, honoring it as **antarmukha**—the inward-turning mystery.

**Powers**: High ability to harmonize Ākṣara through rhythmic movement and mantra. Moderate-high perception of spiritual resonances. Exceptional emotional intelligence.

**Special Powers**: *Samanvaya Drishti* reveals hidden energetic constitution of beings, exposing spiritual gender beyond physical form, but requires intense concentration and leaves the user vulnerable to psychic backlash for one hour afterward. Can manifest *Mithuna-Mandalas*—temporary 15-meter zones where Ākṣara flows in balance, accelerating healing and spiritual growth, but requires at least three Kinnaras working in perfect harmony and collapses if concentration breaks.

**Inspiration**: Ardhanarishvara, Gandharva mythology, hijra communities as spiritual guardians.
