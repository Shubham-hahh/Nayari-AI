# Niyatavādin

**Scientific name**: *Niyatavādin sthiratā*

**Common name:** Niyatavādin 

**Earliest Ancestor**: The *Ākāśa-vastradhārin* (Sky-Clad Guardians) of Svarga's outermost court.

**Type**: Intelligent

**Diet**: Primarily vegetarian, ritualistically prepared. Meat is consumed only during prescribed festivals, and only from animals that died of natural causes.

**Intelligence**: High, with exceptional talent for jurisprudence, architecture, calendrical science, and symbolic logic. Less adaptable to sudden change.

**Appearance:&#x20;**&#x53;mooth, hairless skin with a faint, opalescent sheen, reminiscent of mother-of-pearl. Height ranges from 1.7m (minimum) to 1.9m (maximum). Their most distinctive feature is their **ritual vestment**—a living, bioluminescent membrane called the *Vastra-tvak* that grows from their shoulders and hips. It is not clothing in the traditional sense but a symbiotic organ that responds to emotion and intent, shifting through subtle patterns of light and intricate, embroidered-like textures. It is considered the physical manifestation of their soul's stability and social role. To be without it is the ultimate shame, called *Nirvastratā* (The Unclothed State). Their faces are serene, with large, calm eyes of solid gold or silver, lacking visible pupils.

**Behavior:** Deliberate, formal, and deeply communal. They value predictability, clear hierarchy, and eloquent silence as much as speech. Their humor is dry and ritualized. Public displays of strong emotion are heavily moderated through the *Vastra-tvak*'s patterns.

**Culture:** Centered on the *Niyama-maṇḍala* (Circle of Duty), a complex social and spiritual system governing every aspect of life, from the angle at which one greets a superior to the precise recipes for festival meals. They are master builders, lawyers, and historians, believing that a well-ordered society is the highest tribute to the cosmos from which they fell. Their architecture is famously symmetrical and enduring.

**Age:** 150-250 years (approx.)

**Backstory: I**n the cosmic taxonomy, the Niyatavādin were the **Ākāśa-vastradhārin**, guardians of the threshold between Svarga's crystalline light and the Madhyama realm. Their purpose was to maintain cosmic order by preventing the raw beauty of heaven from destabilizing mortal realms. However, during a philosophical schism known as the *Vicāra-bhaṅga* (Shattering Debate), a faction argued that true order required *participation* in chaos, not mere separation from it. For this heresy of "engaged stability," they were cast out not into Naraka, but into the Kālāntara Void. Their ship-of-exile, a fragment of solidified protocol, crashed into the then-forming continent of **Uttarārdha**. In that harsh, syllabically-frozen environment, their celestial bodies adapted. The *Vastra-tvak* evolved as both protection against the cold and a constant, somatic reminder of the duty and dignity they must uphold. They see themselves not as fallen angels, but as **planted seeds of order** in a chaotic universe, their very existence a testament to the possibility of structured harmony.

**Reproduction:&#x20;**&#x53;exual reproduction between a cisgender male and female, following a highly formalized courtship ritual (*Vivāha-niyati*) that can last up to a year. Conception is only attempted during the **Prāṇotsava** month, aligning the child's first breath with the world's inhalation. Pregnancy is a public, celebrated state where the mother's *Vastra-tvak* grows more radiant and complex. Children are considered community property, raised in communal crèches to instill shared values from infancy.

**Powers:**

* **Ākāṣara Channeling:** Low-to-moderate innate ability, but achieve exceptional precision and endurance through ritual focus and geometric spellcasting (sigils, mandalas). Their magic is slow, powerful, and incredibly stable.
* **Mnemonics:** Perfect cultural and lineal memory. An elder can recite the complete history of their bloodline and all major community events for the past thousand years.
* **Temporal Anchoring:** They naturally stabilize magical and temporal fluctuations in their immediate vicinity. A group of meditating Niyatavādin can calm a localized mana-storm or strengthen a weakening Temporal Anchor.

**Special Powers:**

* **Vastra-Kavaca:** In times of extreme threat, the *Vastra-tvak* can harden into a brilliant, semi-solid armor of light, protecting the wearer. This drains their personal Ākṣara reserves severely and can take months to recover from.
* **Vākya-Siddhi:** The "Perfection of Speech." A promise or vow spoken by a Niyatavādin in the full formal ritual context becomes a mild geas, binding them to its fulfillment. Breaking it causes their *Vastra-tvak* to dim and fray permanently.

**Inspiration:** Confucian scholar-gentlemen, Byzantine court officials, and the concept of *dharma* as cosmic order and duty. The aesthetic of celestial bureaucrats.
