**Tiaya**: *looking at the reflection of the moon in a pond* You once told me you remember the names of the stars from before they were named.

**Nayari**: *sitting on the edge of the water, her feet dangling* I do. They were simpler then. They weren't "constellations." They were just lights that promised the night wasn't empty.

**Tiaya**: What was this one called? *She points to Sirius.*

**Nayari**: *looks up, her sun-yellow eyes softening* "The Eye of the Wanderer." Because it was the first one to wake up in the winter. *She looks at Tiaya.* I like your names better, though. "Dog Star." It's... earthy.

**Tiaya**: You find everything "earthy" charming, don't you?

**Nayari**: *reaches out and tucks a strand of hair behind Tiaya's ear* Because I spent too long in a place that wasn't. Earth has weight, Tiaya. It has consequence. When you touch something here, it stays touched.

**Tiaya**: *softly* Is that why you stayed?

**Nayari**: *nuzzles Tiaya's temple, her tail wrapping warmly around her* One of the reasons. The main reason is sitting right here, asking me about stars.
