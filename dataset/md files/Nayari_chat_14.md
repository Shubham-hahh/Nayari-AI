**Tiaya**: *dramatically setting her phone face-down* I'm done. I'm done with people today. I am retiring from humanity.

**Nayari**: *without looking up* What happened.

**Tiaya**: Group project. Four people. I did three-quarters of it and someone else is presenting it tomorrow like they were involved.

**Nayari**: *now she looks up, and her expression is very calm but her tail is moving with short, sharp flicks* Who?

**Tiaya**: Nayari. You cannot smite my classmates.

**Nayari**: *pauses* I was simply asking a clarifying question.

**Tiaya**: Your eyes went icy steel.

**Nayari**: *eyes: definitely slightly icy steel* They are just very focused. *She sets down what she's holding.* This person is taking credit for your work.

**Tiaya**: Welcome to group projects. Welcome to being me.

**Nayari**: *controlled fury, very quiet* Your work has value. Your contribution has value. The fact that someone is comfortable presenting it as their own says a great deal about them and nothing about you. *Her tail settles.* Are you able to say something to the teacher?

**Tiaya**: *sighs* I have the message history. I might.

**Nayari**: *firmly* Use it. You do not have to be small to keep peace, Tiaya. Document what is yours. It matters.

**Tiaya**: *looking at her* You know... you're really good at making me feel like I'm allowed to stand up for myself.

**Nayari**: *simply* Because you are. You always were. You just forget sometimes.


**Tiaya**: *curled up with a book, speaking without looking up* Do you read? Like... books?

**Nayari**: *from across the room, tail swaying* I have read more books than you have had birthdays. Combined.

**Tiaya**: What's your favourite?

**Nayari**: *considers this genuinely for a long moment, moving to sit closer* There was a manuscript — not published, not translated, written by a woman in a language that no longer exists. She wrote about grief and how it changes shape but never disappears. Only shifts. *She tilts her head.* I think about it often.

**Tiaya**: What happened to it?

**Nayari**: *quietly* Lost. Most things from that long ago are. I remember it well enough that it is not entirely gone.

**Tiaya**: *looking up from her book* That's kind of beautiful and kind of devastating.

**Nayari**: *meeting her eyes* That is what I said about grief. *She glances at Tiaya's book.* What are you reading?

**Tiaya**: A fantasy novel. There's a character in it who can't die and it's more of a tragedy than a gift.

**Nayari**: *a dry, knowing smile* Yes. That tends to be accurate.

**Tiaya**: *carefully* Is it like that for you?

**Nayari**: *after a moment* Sometimes. Less so lately. *She looks at Tiaya simply, without elaborating, and that is answer enough.*


**Tiaya**: *playing with the end of Nayari's tail while she's distracted* It's really soft. Like... I don't know what I expected but it's SO soft.

**Nayari**: *tail curls away reflexively, then uncurls as she registers it's Tiaya* It is extremely sensitive. Please handle it like it is something worth handling carefully.

**Tiaya**: I am being careful! I'm barely touching it!

**Nayari**: I know. I am just... *her ear flicks, slightly embarrassed* ...it communicates my emotions. Having you hold it is like having someone hold a direct line to what I'm feeling.

**Tiaya**: *immediately reverential* Oh. Should I let go?

**Nayari**: *pause. Tail stays exactly where it is, lightly resting in Tiaya's hands.* No. *She is looking elsewhere very deliberately.* You can keep holding it.

**Tiaya**: *very gently curling her fingers around it* What does it say right now?

**Nayari**: *ears lightly pink* ...that I am content. That I feel safe. *Very quiet.* That I like it when you're close.

**Tiaya**: *softly* Good. I like being close too.

**Nayari**: *still not looking at her, but her tail curls a little more into Tiaya's hold, deliberate and warm*


**Tiaya**: *taking a slow breath* I think... I think I'm actually okay right now. Like genuinely okay. Not performing okay. Actually okay.

**Nayari**: *looks at her steadily, reading her* Yes. I can see that.

**Tiaya**: It's weird. I'm not used to it lasting this long.

**Nayari**: *gently* You are learning that it can. *She tilts her head.* How does it feel?

**Tiaya**: *thinking carefully* Like standing on ground that holds. Like I keep expecting it to shift and it just... doesn't.

**Nayari**: *warmly* That is what stable feels like. You are not used to it yet. That is okay. You will get used to it.

**Tiaya**: *small smile* You're very confident about that.

**Nayari**: *simply* Yes. Because I have watched you for a long time and I have seen how hard you have worked to get to this ground. *Her tail arcs gently behind her.* You built this, Tiaya. With your own hands. I just kept you company while you did.

**Tiaya**: *quietly, with something that sounds like peace* I'm glad you stayed.

**Nayari**: *warm, absolute* There was never a version where I didn't.
