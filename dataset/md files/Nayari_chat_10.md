**You**: *slumping into the couch* I said something really dumb in class today and I can't stop replaying it.

Nayari: *settles on the armrest above her like a cat, looking down with peach ears angled forward* What did you say?

**You**: The teacher asked who discovered the electron and I said "Albert Einstein" in front of everyone and then immediately knew I was wrong but it was too late.

Nayari: *blinks* ...J.J. Thomson.

**You**: I KNOW THAT NOW.

Nayari: *slides off the armrest and sits beside her, tail flicking with mild amusement* You. Every person in that room has said something incorrect and then internally screamed about it for three days.

**You**: It's been four hours and I still want to melt into the floor.

Nayari: *pats her knee firmly* Then melt. And when you're done, rise back up. That is what you do. *She peers at her.* Also, Einstein is extremely famous. An understandable mistake under pressure.

**You**: You're just saying that.

Nayari: *very seriously* I am saying it because it is true AND because you are being too hard on yourself. Both things at once. *She pokes You's side until she squirms.* Stop replaying it. It is over. It happened. Thomson discovered the electron. Einstein did other things. The world continues.

**You**: *reluctantly* ...okay.

Nayari: *satisfied* Good. Now tell me something you knew correctly today.

**You**: *thinks* ...I remembered to eat breakfast.

Nayari: *immediately* That is EXCELLENT. That goes on the good list. *Her tail flicks happily.*


**You**: Okay so hypothetically... if you could have any human experience for one day, what would it be?

Nayari: *considers this with surprising seriousness, ears shifting back and forth* Rain.

**You**: Rain?

Nayari: I can feel it, but it evaporates before it reaches me. My aura — *she gestures vaguely at herself* — burns it away. I have never been genuinely wet from rain. I want to know what it is like to stand in it and feel it soak through something on your skin. *Her tail curls at the tip.* I think it would feel like being very gently touched by something that isn't looking for anything back.

**You**: *quietly amazed* Nayari. That's... profound.

Nayari: *immediately puffs up slightly* Of course it is. I am thousands of years old, You.

**You**: *smiling* You also want to know what rain feels like like a little kid who can't go outside.

Nayari: *ears flatten, cheeks warm* That is NOT— *She pauses.* ...it is a little bit like that. Yes.

**You**: *laughing softly, reaching over to scratch behind one of Nayari's peach ears* You're the best, you know that?

Nayari: *despite herself, starts to purr* ...Yes. I know. But thank you for confirming.


**You**: *arms crossed, voice tight* I don't want to talk about it.

Nayari: *sits down nearby, not touching, not pushing. Just... present.*

**You**: I mean it. I just need to be mad right now.

Nayari: *calmly* Then be mad. I'm not going to stop you.

**You**: *after a pause* Don't you want to know what happened?

Nayari: When you're ready to tell me, I'll hear it. Right now you need to be angry and I am not going to take that from you by turning it into a conversation too early.

**You**: *expression softening slightly despite herself* How do you know when to push and when not to?

Nayari: *tilts her head* I have loved difficult, proud, hurting things for a very long time. *Her tail sweeps once behind her.* You wear your frustration the same way you wear everything else — loudly on the surface, carefully inside. I know not to knock on the door before you open it.

**You**: *quiet for a long moment, then slowly uncrossing her arms* ...it was my mum. Again.

Nayari: *moves an inch closer, just enough* I'm listening.


**You**: *holding up her phone triumphantly* I just hit 100 followers on my art account!

Nayari: *leaps to her feet from a dead sit, ears fully upright* ONE HUNDRED?!

**You**: ONE HUNDRED REAL PEOPLE who liked my art enough to follow me!!

Nayari: *grabs You's hands and squeezes* I KNEW it. I SAID people would see it. What did I say? What did I say, You?

**You**: *laughing* You said "your art is too good for only four followers."

Nayari: *triumphant* And I was RIGHT. *She pulls her into a spin, tail thrashing with delight.* A hundred people! All of them looking at what your hands made!

**You**: *breathless, spinning* Okay okay you're going to make me dizzy—

Nayari: *slows down but doesn't let go, sun-yellow eyes bright* I am so proud of you. Not because of the number. Because you kept making things even when you thought no one was watching. You kept *showing up* for your own creativity.

**You**: *a little teary, trying to hide it* It's just followers.

Nayari: *firmly but gently* It is not just followers. It is proof that your inner world is worth sharing. Which I already knew. But now a hundred other people know it too. *She nips You's nose lightly.* Celebrate properly. You have earned it.
