# Technical Overview of the Ocean

## I. Physical & Geographical Profile

### Basic Statistics

* **Name**: Aryabhatta Ocean (named for the Earth astronomer whose calculations accidentally described its tides)
* **Classification**: Singular Global Ocean (Pangaeic Ocean)
* **Surface Area**: 34.68 billion km² (3.468 × 10¹⁰ km², covers 68.0% of planetary surface)
* **Average Depth**: 4.5 kilometers
* **Maximum Depth**: 11.2 kilometers (in the Sapta-Garta Trench)
* **Volume**: \~1.56 × 10¹¹ km³
* **Salinity**: Variable (3.2-3.8%) based on Ākṣara concentration
* **Temperature Range**: -1.8°C (polar) to 32°C (equatorial)

### Major Basins & Subdivisions

1. **Asiat Basin** (Northern Tropical): 8.0 billion km², average depth 3.5 km
2. **Dakṣiṇa Depression** (Southern Temperate): 12.0 billion km², average depth 5.5 km
3. **Himavantic Abyss** (Polar Northern): 5.5 billion km², average depth 6.5 km
4. **Brahmapurī Shallows** (Equatorial): 4.0 billion km², average depth 1.5 km
5. **Mārutadeśa Marginal Sea** (Arid Zone): 2.0 billion km², hypersaline (4.2%), average depth 3.0 km
6. **Dvipa Antara Archipelagic Complex**: 3.2 billion km², highly variable depth, average 4.5 km

### Seafloor Topography

* **Mid-Oceanic Ridges**: Mantra-Ridge System (32,000 km continuous)
* **Deep-Sea Trenches**:
  * Sapta-Garta Trench (11.2 km deep, near Dakṣiṇa Bhūmi)
  * Rudra's Chasm (9.8 km deep, polar region)
* **Hydrothermal Vent Fields**: Ākṣara-Vents (87 major fields)
* **Abyssal Plains**: Vast, flat regions covered in syllabic sediment
* **Seamounts**: Fossilized mantra-mountains (1,247 cataloged)

## II. Oceanographic Systems

### Current Systems

* **Primary Gyre**:
  * Southern Hemisphere: Clockwise circulation
  * Northern Hemisphere: Counter-clockwise circulation
  * Speed: 0.5-2.0 m/s
  * Width: 200-500 km
* **Ākṣara Conveyor**:
  * Type: Deep cold current
  * Function: Transports fossilized Ākṣara from poles to equator
  * Depth: 2,000-4,000 m
  * Temperature: 1-4°C
  * Special Property: Revives ancient syllables at upwelling zones
* **Mana Currents**:
  * Surface streams charged with solar wind particulates
  * Flow westward as "light-rivers"
  * Navigable by Kinnara pilots
  * Shift after every Teja-stambha flare

### Tidal Dynamics

* **Primary Influence**: Śālagrīva (moon)
* **Tidal Range**: Up to 18 meters (highest in Brahmapurī region)
* **Daily Pattern**: Three peaks (Aśva-ghāta) due to Preta-Loka interference
* **40-Day Cycle**: Extreme tides at perigee create sacred springs
* **Tidal Bore**: Mantra-Bore in Brahmapurī delta (8 m high, travels 30 km inland)

### Water Properties

* **Ākṣara Concentration**: 0.1-3.7 ppm (parts per million)
* **Density Gradient**: Increases with Ākṣara content (1.020-1.035 g/cm³)
* **Bioluminescence**: 87% of deep-sea species produce light
* **Acoustic Properties**: Water transmits mantras clearly over long distances
* **Optical Properties**: Violet-gold refraction at high Ākṣara concentrations
