# Abyssopelagic Zone (Mūla-Śūnya Layer)

## Geographic Definition & Boundaries

**Depth Range**: 4,000-6,000 meters (the true abyss)

**Spatial Extent**: Occupies **40% of ocean floor area** (13.9 billion km²) and 22% of ocean volume. Lower boundary defined by transition to hadal trench morphology (>6,000m).

**Geographic Positioning**: Plains beyond continental rise, exclusively oceanic crust, oldest seafloor regions.

## Physical Geography

**Extreme Pressure**:

* **4,000m**: 401 atmospheres
* **6,000m**: 601 atmospheres
* **Compressibility Effects**: Water volume reduces by 2.3% relative to surface, increasing density to 1.059 g/cm³

**Uniform Cold**: 1-3°C throughout, with thermal stratification eliminated by extreme pressure

**Complete Stasis**: Current velocities 0.01-0.05 m/s, creating nearly motionless water masses

## Geological Features

**Abyssal Plain Morphology**:

* **Ultra-Flat Topography**: Relief <10m over 100 km distances
* **Syllabic Ripple Fields**: Wavelength 50-200m, amplitude 0.5-1.5m, oriented by Coriolis forces
* **Prāṇa Scour Pits**: 10-30m diameter, 2-5m depth, formed by spirit vortices

**Sediment Cover**:

* **Thickness**: 500-1,200m of unconsolidated syllabic sediment
* **Age**: Up to 180 million years at base (Jurassic equivalent period)
* **Composition**: 70% biogenic micro-syllabic shells, 20% authigenic clays, 10% cosmic particles

**Trench Proximity Effects**:

* **Sapta-Garta Trench Rim**: Plains slope upward at 0.2° within 20 km of trench
* **Slump Deposits**: 40% increased sediment thickness within 50 km of trench scarps

## Hydrological Geography

**Stagnant Water Masses**: Abyssopelagic water has residence time of 2,000-3,000 years, oldest on planet.

**Sediment-Water Interface**:

* **Boundary Layer**: 1-2m thick, enriched in resuspended particles
* **Pore Water Flow**: Upward diffusion of Ākṣara from sediment at rate of 0.1 mm/year

**Vent Influence**: Minimal except near ridge axes; 95% of this zone is biologically inactive plain.

## Biogeographic Patterns

**Geographic Distribution of Life**:

* **Biomass**: <1 gram per m² (lowest of any zone)
* **Patchiness**: 90% of seafloor completely barren; life concentrated in:
  * Turbidite deposit zones (0.1% of area)
  * Whale fall sites (temporary oases)
  * Syllabic sediment "hotspots" with elevated Ākṣara (0.5-1.0 ppm)

**Vetala Survey Grids**: Mapped as 100 km × 100 km cells, each requiring 40-day expedition to characterize.

## Geographic Formation Processes

**Sedimentation Rate**: 0.5-2 mm per 1,000 years, slowest on planet due to distance from land.

**Syllabic Stratigraphy**:

* Each 1 mm layer records approximately 100-200 years
* Complete planetary history preserved in readable sequence
* **Ramanuja's Archive**: Sedimentary sequence from 4,000-6,000m in Dakṣiṇa Depression contains unbroken record of last 180 million years

**Tectonic Stability**: Seafloor spreading rates 2-4 cm/year, preserving ancient sediments without subduction.

## Exploration & Mapping

**Survey Coverage**: Only **8% of abyssopelagic zone directly explored**

**Vetala Deep-Mapping Protocols**:

* **Grid Spacing**: 500 km between survey lines
* **Technology**: Ākṣara-echo sounding, pressure-resistant scrying crystals
* **Challenges**: 601 atm pressure requires specialized vessels; magic behaves unpredictably

**Geographic Knowledge Gaps**:

* **Himavantic Abyssal Plain**: 88% unmapped due to ice cover preventing surface support
