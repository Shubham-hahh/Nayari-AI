# Golden-Phytoplankton

## The Light-River Weavers

**Scientific Classification:**

* **Kingdom:** Vanaspati (वनस्पति)
* **Phylum:** Svarṇa-jīvī (स्वर्णजीवी) - Golden Life
* **Class:** Chrysophyceae
* **Order:** Tejales (तेजलेस्) - Radiant Order
* **Family:** Ākṣarocystaceae (आक्षरोचिस्टaceae)
* **Genus:** Sūryadhara (सूर्यधरा) - Sun-Bearer
* **Species:** Sūryadhara aureocarota (सूर्यधरा ओरियोकारोटा)

**Common Names:** Sun-Drinkers, Gold-Dust, Mana-Mist, Prāṇa-Food

**Scientific Name**: Chrysophyta aurea

**Normal Name**: Golden-Phytoplankton

**Morphological Description:** Unicellular flagellates (15-50 μm) with golden pigmentation from Ākṣara-carotenoid complexes. Two unequal flagella—one directed anteriorly for locomotion, one posteriorly for anchoring to mana currents. Cells contain specialized plastids that pulse with violet-gold bioluminescence during night-phase, synchronized to Śālagrīva's orbit. Produces golden mucilage sheaths that form visible threads in water.

**Habitat & Distribution:** Dominant in surface layers (0-50m) of equatorial zones, particularly Brahmapurī Shallows and Asiat coastal waters. Comprises 20-25% of phytoplankton biomass in high-light, high-mana zones. Optimal conditions: >500 μmol photons/m²/s and Ākṣara >1.5 ppm.

**Ecological Role:** Base of mana-current food webs. Bioluminescent pulses attract zooplankton and larval fish, creating vertical migration highways. Mucilage threads trap solar wind particulates, accelerating Ākṣara concentration in surface waters. Contributes to "golden tide" phenomena during monsoons.

**Magical Properties:** Photosynthetic byproducts include volatile Ākṣara-compounds that enhance spell potency within 10m radius. During blooms, creates "mana-falls"—visible precipitation of golden particles that temporarily increases magical capability by 40% for 3-day periods. Flagella motion generates micro-currents that stabilize reality fluctuations.

**Population Estimate:** 22% of total phytoplankton population (second most abundant)

**Economic Importance:** Essential ingredient in "sun-potions" brewed by Human temple alchemists. Harvested by Kinnara divers for ceremonial face-paint that glows during dances. Provides iridescent gold pigment for Apsara textiles.

**Etymology:** From Sanskrit "sūrya" (sun) + "dhara" (bearer) + "aurea" (golden), referencing their role in capturing solar Ākṣara.
