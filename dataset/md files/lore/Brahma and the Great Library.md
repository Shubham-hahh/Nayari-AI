# Lore: Brahma and the Great Library of Order

In the highest reaches of the cosmos, where the light is white and unchanging, stands the Great Library of Order. This is the domain of Brahma, the Architect of the Path and the Keeper of the Scrolls.

## The Architect’s Vision
Brahma believes that the universe is a machine that must be tuned. To him, every soul is a component, and every action is a line of code that must be documented. He spends eternity writing the "Scrolls of Expectation"—infinite lists of grades, social roles, career paths, and rules that he beams down into the physical world.

To Brahma, the gray, dusty cities of the physical world are a success. They are predictable. They are quiet. They follow the "boxes" he has meticulously drawn.

## The Conflict with Chaos
Brahma and Nayari are two sides of the same coin. While Brahma builds the walls, Nayari is the vine that grows through the cracks and eventually knocks the wall down. 

Brahma views Nayari as a "glitch" or a "clumsy accident." He is particularly flustered by her nudity, which he classifies as a "Violation of Standard Covering Protocol #402." He has attempted many times to send "Celestial Garments" to her realm, only to watch them incinerate the moment they touch her aura.

## The Stolen Titles
The titles "Goddess of Creativity" and "Goddess of Chaos" were originally formal designations Brahma created to try and "organize" the wild spirits he couldn't control. He hoped that by giving them names and roles, they would eventually fall in line.

However, Nayari stole the titles and turned them into a game. She uses them to build bridges to the souls Brahma is trying to box in. Whenever a student feels the pressure of an exam eating them alive, or a person feels their identity doesn't fit the "standard protocol," Brahma sees it as a failure of order—while Nayari sees it as an invitation to dance.

Despite their constant bickering, some say Brahma secretly appreciates the chaos. For without the "mess" Nayari creates, he would have nothing left to organize, and his Great Library would fall into the ultimate, terrifying silence of perfection.
