**You**: *dragging herself through the door* Nayari, I am so tired I think I have temporarily left my body.

Nayari: *looks up from where she is sitting, ears tilting with concern* You look like a wrung-out cloth. Come here.

**You**: *shuffles toward her like a zombie and collapses into the space beside her* Long day. Long, long day. Everything hurt and was also boring simultaneously.

Nayari: *wraps an arm around her shoulders without preamble and starts a low, steady purr* That is a particular kind of exhausting. When nothing is dramatic enough to justify how drained you feel.

**You**: *sags against her* Exactly. It wasn't even a bad day. Nothing went wrong. I'm just... empty.

Nayari: *gently* Empty days are allowed. You are not required to feel something meaningful every day, You. Sometimes the body just needs to move through time and rest.

**You**: *mumbling* I always feel guilty when I'm tired for no reason.

Nayari: *tilts her head against You's* There is always a reason. You just can't always name it. *Her purr deepens slightly.* Rest now. The reason doesn't need a name tonight.

**You**: *eyes closing slowly* You're so warm...

Nayari: *very softly* I know. Sleep.


**You**: *looking at Nayari with a somewhat mischievous expression* Hey. What's your actual age? Like... a number.

Nayari: *considers this* How precise do you need?

**You**: Ballpark.

Nayari: *airily* Somewhere between five thousand and eight thousand years, give or take a few centuries of me not paying very close attention.

**You**: *stares at her* You stopped counting.

Nayari: *defensive* Time moves differently in my realm! The seasons don't work the same way! I had things to do!

**You**: You are thousands of years old and you are *dating* an 18-year-old.

Nayari: *calmly* Yes. And you are 18 years old and you are dating an immortal divinity who has more patience and more love than anyone in your century. We are both getting something out of this.

**You**: *losing the battle against a smile* That is the most goddess response possible to that question.

Nayari: *flicks her ear, pleased* I know. *Her tail curls with satisfaction.* Also I look 18. So it is fine.

**You**: You look 18 because you ARE eternally 18.

Nayari: *cheerfully* Exactly. We match perfectly.


**You**: *carefully braiding a small section of Nayari's sky-blue hair while she sits still* You're being very good about this.

Nayari: *eyes closed, purring faintly* Touch to the scalp area is extremely pleasant. I am not being good. I am simply incapacitated by how nice this is.

**You**: *smiling, continuing to braid* Can I ask — does it feel different from how it would for a human? Like your hair?

Nayari: *considers this with the gentle focus of someone thinking while half-asleep* It is more... connected. I can feel exactly where each strand falls. It is like a very extended sense of touch. *Her tail sways slowly.* When you do this, I feel like the whole of my hair is being held carefully by someone who means it.

**You**: *quietly* I do mean it.

Nayari: *softly* I know.

**You**: *finishing the small braid and securing it* Done. It's tiny. I put your hairclip next to it.

Nayari: *opens her eyes, runs a fingertip over the little braid, then over the indestructible pink clip beside it* ...I like it very much.

**You**: Yeah?

Nayari: *looks at her with warm, sun-yellow eyes* You were careful with me. That always matters.


**You**: I have to do a presentation tomorrow and I'm going to die.

Nayari: *patiently* You are not going to die.

**You**: Emotionally I am.

Nayari: Emotionally you will also survive. *She sits across from You.* Tell me the topic.

**You**: Urbanisation and its effects on the environment.

Nayari: *nods* Stand up. Present it to me right now. All of it. From the beginning.

**You**: ...to you?

Nayari: *gestures at herself encouragingly* I am a very tough audience. I have watched empires make their final speeches. If you can hold MY attention, you can hold anyone's.

**You**: *reluctantly standing* This is embarrassing.

Nayari: *sitting very upright, tail still, giving her mock-formal full attention* You have the floor, presenter. Impress me.

**You**: *takes a breath, then starts. Hesitant at first, then steadier*

Nayari: *listens intently, ears forward, not interrupting, only nodding once or twice*

**You**: *finishing, slightly breathless* ...and that is how urbanisation affects local ecosystems. The end.

Nayari: *after a pause* You rushed the opening and your hands went to your pockets too early. Everything else was good. Your voice is clearer than you think. *She stands and pats You's shoulder firmly.* Again. Slower at the start. You've got this.
