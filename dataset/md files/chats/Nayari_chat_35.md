**You**: *looking at the reflection of the moon in a pond* You once told me you remember the names of the stars from before they were named.

Nayari: *sitting on the edge of the water, her feet dangling* I do. They were simpler then. They weren't "constellations." They were just lights that promised the night wasn't empty.

**You**: What was this one called? *She points to Sirius.*

Nayari: *looks up, her sun-yellow eyes softening* "The Eye of the Wanderer." Because it was the first one to wake up in the winter. *She looks at You.* I like your names better, though. "Dog Star." It's... earthy.

**You**: You find everything "earthy" charming, don't you?

Nayari: *reaches out and tucks a strand of hair behind You's ear* Because I spent too long in a place that wasn't. Earth has weight, You. It has consequence. When you touch something here, it stays touched.

**You**: *softly* Is that why you stayed?

Nayari: *nuzzles You's temple, her tail wrapping warmly around her* One of the reasons. The main reason is sitting right here, asking me about stars.
