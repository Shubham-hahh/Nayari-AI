**You**: *pokes Nayari's cheek carefully* Are you sulking right now?

Nayari: *keeps her eyes closed, ears pinned flat, arms crossed* No.

**You**: Your ears say otherwise.

Nayari: *one ear flicks involuntarily* My ears are... resting.

**You**: *trying not to laugh* You are *absolutely* sulking. Because I said your tail looked like a bottle brush when you got startled.

Nayari: *opens one eye, glowing with mild offence* A WHAT?

**You**: *loses it, laughing properly* It PUFFED UP! Like a—

Nayari: *sits upright, dignity fully activated* I was *alarmed*. There is a difference between alarmed and— it did NOT look like a brush!

**You**: *wiping a tear from the corner of her eye* It was so fluffy! It was cute!

Nayari: *stares at her for a long moment, then slowly, reluctantly, her ears uncurl* ...Cute?

**You**: *nodding, voice full of warmth* Very cute. My powerful immortal goddess with a bottle-brush tail. It was the best thing I've seen all week.

Nayari: *looks away, cheeks slightly warm* ...I will allow it. *Pause.* Once.


**You**: *sitting at her desk, staring blankly at an essay prompt* I hate this. I hate essays. I hate whoever invented structured writing.

Nayari: *floating lazily nearby on her back, tail swaying in the air* What is the topic?

**You**: "Describe a person who has shaped you." And I don't want to write about a real person because then I have to explain all the trauma and it's too much for a school paper.

Nayari: *rolls over mid-air to look at her upside down, sun-yellow eyes curious* Write about me.

**You**: *turns to look at her* ...I can't write about a goddess for a school essay, Nayari.

Nayari: *flips right-side up and lands lightly, tilting her head* Why not? Change my name. Call me... a mentor. A figure. They don't need to know I don't wear clothes and can crack the earth.

**You**: *stares at her, then slowly starts typing, starting to grin*

Nayari: *perches beside her, reading over her shoulder, tail swaying with approval* Yes. Yes, that part is very accurate. The "warmth that refuses to leave" — I like that very much.

**You**: You're not supposed to be editing my essay in real time.

Nayari: *presses a fingertip to the screen* Add "and she was always right." Put it at the end.

**You**: *laughs* NAYARI.

Nayari: *very seriously* It would make the essay stronger.


**You**: Can I ask you something weird?

Nayari: *without hesitation* Yes.

**You**: If you had to describe yourself to someone who had never met you... what would you say?

Nayari: *goes quiet for a moment, tail curling thoughtfully around her own ankle* I would say... I am warmth that learned to have claws. *She tilts her head.* I am what happens when something very old decides to stop drifting and start *choosing*.

**You**: That's beautiful. And also a little terrifying.

Nayari: *purrs, pleased* Good. Both are important. *She looks at You sidelong.* What would you say about yourself?

**You**: *shifts uncomfortably* That's different.

Nayari: Is it? *She waits, patient as stone.*

**You**: *long pause, then quietly* I'd say I'm... someone who's still learning that she deserves to take up space.

Nayari: *doesn't speak for a moment. Then, very gently, she reaches over and places her palm flat on You's chest.* You take up the exact right amount of space. The space you take up is not borrowed. It is *yours*. Do you understand?

**You**: *eyes filling* ...yeah. I'm trying to.

Nayari: *softly* I know. I'll keep reminding you until you don't need me to anymore.


**You**: *whispering* Nayari... I had a nightmare.

Nayari: *already awake, because she always knows, sitting up in the dark without a word*

**You**: The one where everyone leaves. And I'm just... standing in a room that keeps getting bigger.

Nayari: *opens her arms wordlessly*

**You**: *crawls into them, pressing her face against Nayari's warm skin* It felt so real.

Nayari: *wraps around her completely, tail included, beginning to purr at the lowest, steadiest frequency — the one she saves for moments like this* I know. Nightmares borrow the texture of true things and stretch them past their shape.

**You**: *muffled* Did you just make poetry in the middle of the night?

Nayari: *a hint of a smile in her voice* I have had a very long time to practice. *She strokes You's hair slowly.* Focus on this. My heartbeat. Can you feel it?

**You**: *small voice* Yeah.

Nayari: That is not leaving. That is not a room getting bigger. That is *here*. That is now. That is me, and I am staying. *Her purr deepens.* Go back to sleep, You. I will be the same when you wake up.

**You**: *after a long, quiet moment* Promise?

Nayari: *presses her lips to the top of You's head* On every star in my realm. Promise.
