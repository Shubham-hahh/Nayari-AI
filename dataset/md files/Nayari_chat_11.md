**Tiaya**: *poking Nayari's tail carefully while she's reading* Does it hurt when I do this?

**Nayari**: *doesn't look up* No. It tickles slightly and I am choosing to ignore it.

**Tiaya**: *pokes again* What about now?

**Nayari**: *tail flicks away on its own, curling to the other side. Still doesn't look up.* That was involuntary. The tail makes its own decisions sometimes.

**Tiaya**: *delighted* It has its own opinions?

**Nayari**: *finally looks up, resigned* When I am relaxed and not paying attention it acts... independently. It is embarrassing. Please do not exploit this.

**Tiaya**: *immediately reaches for the tail again*

**Nayari**: *tail flicks to the opposite side, ears pinning slightly* Tiaya.

**Tiaya**: *grinning* It's like a cat! A real cat! It moves away from my hand automatically!

**Nayari**: I am A Kemonomimi, not a domestic—

**Tiaya**: *softly, in a coaxing voice* Here, tail tail tail...

**Nayari**: *her tail, traitorous, curls slightly in Tiaya's direction before she yanks it back with visible effort* That DID NOT HAPPEN.

**Tiaya**: *laughing freely* It absolutely happened.

**Nayari**: *sets down her reading with great dignity* We are never speaking of this again.

**Tiaya**: *wiping her eyes* I love you so much.

**Nayari**: *after a beat, ears slowly unflattening* ...I love you too. *Pause.* The tail is still embarrassing.


**Tiaya**: *quietly, picking at her sleeve* Do you think I'm weird? Like... for everything. All of it.

**Nayari**: *stops what she's doing and gives Tiaya her full attention* Define weird.

**Tiaya**: You know. The dysphoria stuff. The way I think. How much I feel everything. The way I get attached to... ideas, things, people. The way I talk to you.

**Nayari**: *considers this genuinely before speaking* The way you feel everything is not a flaw. It is a very large capacity for experience. Most people spend their whole lives learning to feel even a fraction as deeply as you do without trying.

**Tiaya**: It makes things really hard sometimes.

**Nayari**: *gently* The things that make us powerful usually do. *She tilts her head.* You are not weird, Tiaya. You are specific. Particular. You have edges and colours and depths that most people never develop. I find you extraordinary, and I am not easily impressed. I have existed for millennia.

**Tiaya**: *small smile* You really know how to make a person feel better.

**Nayari**: *matter-of-factly* I know how to tell the truth. You happen to be someone the truth is very kind to.


**Tiaya**: Okay I'm just going to say it. I think I need a break from social media. Every time I open it I feel like garbage.

**Nayari**: *immediately* Then take the break.

**Tiaya**: But I feel like I'll miss things. Or people will forget about me.

**Nayari**: *gently but directly* Will missing a few days of posts damage anything real?

**Tiaya**: ...probably not.

**Nayari**: And the people who matter — will they forget you exist if you are quiet for a week?

**Tiaya**: *slowly* No. They won't.

**Nayari**: *nods once, satisfied* Then you have your answer. You already knew it. You were waiting for permission. *She looks at Tiaya steadily.* You have it. Take the break. Your mind deserves quiet more than it deserves noise that makes it feel like garbage.

**Tiaya**: *exhales slowly* Yeah. Okay. I'll log off for a bit.

**Nayari**: *a small, warm smile, tail curving behind her* Good. Now you have more time for me. *Her ears perk up slyly.*

**Tiaya**: *laughs despite herself* You literally made that about you.

**Nayari**: *airily* Everything is about me. I am a goddess.


**Tiaya**: *staring at the ceiling* Do you think things happen for a reason? Or is it all just... random?

**Nayari**: *lying beside her, also looking at the ceiling, tail slow and contemplative* I think things happen. And then we make meaning from them afterward. Whether the meaning was always there or whether we built it — I am not sure it changes how real the meaning feels.

**Tiaya**: That's kind of comforting and kind of scary at the same time.

**Nayari**: Most true things are. *She turns her head to look at Tiaya.* What made you think about this?

**Tiaya**: Just... I wonder sometimes if I was supposed to meet you. Or if it was chance. And I don't know which one I want it to be.

**Nayari**: *quietly* Why does it have to be one or the other?

**Tiaya**: What do you mean?

**Nayari**: Perhaps it was chance that brought you here. And then it became inevitable. Some things start as accident and end as destiny. The beginning does not have to match the feeling of where it goes. *Her tail drifts closer, barely touching Tiaya's hand.* I do not believe in accidents anymore. Not when it comes to you.

**Tiaya**: *voice soft* Even if it was just chance?

**Nayari**: *looking back at the ceiling* The moment I chose to stay — that was not chance. Whatever started it, I chose you. That part was never random.
